I have loved programming since I was a kid. I loved writing code more than playing Lego. — And far more than playing outside with others, but that’s another story.

Lately, my disinterest in keeping up to date with the frenzy about ever-changing JavaScript frameworks and trends, and my growing interest in subjects like product design, growth hacking and bootstrapping makes me understand that what I really care about is creating products. Programming is not an end, it’s just a mean. Even though I’ve been enjoy using that mean!

Given the current state of the software engineering market, and my passion and experience in this domain, I could become very rich and “successful” quite fast, if I wanted.

But my objective is not to become rich. My objective is to be happy.

And I’m happy when I don’t have to take orders from anyone, and free to practice my passion: crafting my own stuff, working for hours in flow mode, and be proud of efficiently reaching a tangible goal, by taking pragmatic technical decisions. And when other people too love what I have done, I feel super-happy!

ACTIVITIES — At this point, here what I’m doing:

  • coding 75% of the time;
  • reading about product development, open-source project coordination, and one-to-one communication;
  • sharing product ideas;
  • reading and replying to user feedback.

GOALS — Here is what I want to achieve:

  • Build software tools that are useful to me and to people who experience similar problems. Why? Because I love to solve problems, and I’m more motivated in solving a problem I’m experiencing.
  • Maintain a relationship with my users, to make sure that my products work great for them. Why? Because I need to feel that what I do is useful for other people than myself, and because I know that being respected (or even admired) is something I strive for.
  • Work on my own, to remain independent, free to do whatever I feel like doing, when and where I want, at all times. Why? Because I hate doing like everybody does, I hate being stuck in crowds (e.g. daily commute at rush hour), waiting (e.g. buy stuff when shops are packed, on weekends and after working hours), I’m very sensitive to distractions (e.g. people playing table tennis in your open space office), and I don’t want to beg anyone for permission before travelling, visiting a close one, or wasting a day on Youtube if I feel like it.
  • Make a living out of this activity, without having to compromise on my real motivation for working on projects (i.e. working on others’ project for money). Why? Well, because I have to pay for my mortgage, taxes, food, travel and leisure.

ASSETS — Here is what I’m grateful to have, today:

  • my loved Macbook Pro;
  • 23k€ sitting on a bank account, that can help me sustain comfortably during 6 months without having to work for anyone else than myself;
  • a free co-working space where I love to work: my own apartment;
  • all my ongoing projects listed on a Trello board, full of cards and checklists of next steps;
  • 3+ fully-functional side-projects: Openwhyd, Next Step, 1poll;
  • a good following: almost 2000 on Twitter, 1200 on Medium, 890 Facebook friends, 400 on ProductHunt;
  • 100 subscribers on my newsletter about productivity, and 8 on my newsletter about side-projects;
  • a loving and supporting girlfriend;
  • ex-employers, clients and friends who trust me, acknowledge my software development skills, and enjoyed working with me;
  • a few close friends who are experienced about startups and/or software development, and know how to cut the bullshit when giving advice;
  • and I’m part of a community of 85 talented and benevolent professionals openly exchanging ideas and kindly giving feedback on Slack.

After 1 month of complete freedom (i.e. no clients, nor engagements of any sort towards other people), working on my own projects, here is where I’m at:

  • I’m getting a bit of interest (thru Pitchcard) and feedback (thru my newsletter) on my product ideas,
  • …but I’m still far away for mass adoption, and have no plan on how to earn income from the work I’ve done.
